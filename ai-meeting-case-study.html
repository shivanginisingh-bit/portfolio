Problem statement

How might we design a cohesive, compliant, and AI-powered meeting summarization experience for 290K+ employees, ensuring seamless integration, inclusivity, and adaptability across diverse scenarios and personas?

Meeting summary experience flow

Join meeting

Employees can join regular meetings and start summarization with standardized messaging informed by device parity analysis, heuristics, and service blueprinting.

Get notified

Streamlined notifications deliver summaries based on user preferences and channel choice, informed by workshops and policy constraints.

Meeting portal

Compliance-focused portal for accessing, searching, and sharing meeting insights while maintaining regulatory standards.

Agentic access

Future on-the-go access, one-click sharing, templates, and in-house LLM agent tools to assist across integrated applications.

Service blueprinting layered with heuristics analysis

Continuous heuristic evaluations were conducted at multiple stages—both during the initial design phase and while reviewing the UI pilot—to enhance the overall experience and reduce user pain points.

Ideation & outcomes

Post UAT testing, and gathering interview insights themes emerged towards personalisation, seamless integration and options for users to review and quick share summaries.

AI model capabilities testing

I conducted speech and scenario testing to assess the AI summarization model's compliance and inclusivity, recruiting participants from different regions and testing across varied meeting types. The results showed fair inclusivity but were indicative rather than conclusive due to the uncontrolled environment. The model demonstrated strong compliance by not capturing sensitive information, but also revealed limitations in handling monologues and to mitigate this we suggested the use of different LLM which was more detailed as well.

Usability findings & synthesis

Through post-meeting pilot usability testing and interviews conducted by the research team, along with portal usage data, weekly feedback, and workshops with the product team, I gathered insights that informed future features—including quick sharing, integrating in-house LLM capabilities, and improved search and filtering for the next version of the portal.

Visual Design & thought process

All screens are either recreated or blurred to comply with NDA. Sensitive information has been anonymized, and the visuals are intended solely to illustrate design thinking and evolution.

01

Initial Design Hypothesis

Envision an end-to-end intelligent meeting summarisation experience.

  • Integrated quick AI toolbox for context actions.
  • Search & filter as core navigation.
  • Attendees & action items surfaced up front.
02

Post Feasibility & MVP Prioritisation

Align with PM & Eng on feasible V1 and critical path features.

  • Prioritised "new summary" and "download" as critical path features.
  • Deferred advanced search/filter to later validation.
  • Scoped interaction to single-page summary view to accelerate delivery.
03

User Interviews & Usability Feedback

Validate V1 experience; identify usability gaps.

  • High value in filters, meeting type, and quick sharing.
  • Need fast retrieval and preference controls.
04

Integrating Insights (Day 2 / V2 Evolution)

Evolve design through evidence-based iteration.

  • Introduced Preference Settings panel within the portal (Day 2).
  • Integrated Quick Share flows into both the summary view and AI toolbox.
  • Adopted floating search/filter & AI toolbox to balance flexibility with compliance limitations.

Solution Highlights

  • AI-generated meeting summaries portal for central access.
  • Scenario testing framework to improve summary quality.
  • Multi-device consistency for seamless adoption.
  • Action-item notifications for accountability.

Key learnings/Reflections

  • Designing AI-first experiences while balancing trust and usability.
  • Scaling enterprise adoption via cross-functional alignment.
  • Rapid iteration, pilot launches, measurable metrics for adoption.
